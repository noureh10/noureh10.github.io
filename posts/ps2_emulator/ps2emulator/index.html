Introduction

Have you ever wanted to play some sweet classics such as “Jak X : Combat Racing” or “Ratchet and Clank” ? But your find yourself with your back against the wall because you don’t own a PS2 or find it non convenient? Well fear no more ! Here we’ll discuss about PCSX2, an open-source software that let you play PS2 games on your computer.

Before installing this piece of software, let’s talk about the minimum requirements as well as the prerequisites.

Minimum requirements

Operating system : Windows 8.1 or highter / Ubuntu 18.04 or higher.

CPU : SSE4.1 support -> anything above the Silvermont micro-architecture for Intel and K10-based micro-architecture for AMD.

Memory : 4 GB RAM

Graphics hardware : Anything DX10 or OpenGL 3.x capable with at least 2GB VRAM.

Prerequisites

You’ll will need two things before installing the emulator :

  • BIOS file
  • PS2 ROM

The BIOS is required to start the emulator and to play games .The BIOS region need to match the game region due to regional lockout, for example you will need a PAL BIOS for European copies and NTSC BIOS for American copies.

There are two ways of obtaining a BIOS:

  • Dumping your BIOS
  • Downloading it
Dumping your BIOS

Dumping your PS2 bios seems like a tedious process reserved for tech savvy people but it’s really not. There are many ways to achieve it :

  • Using a FMCB Memory card (purchasable here)
  • Using Swap Magic disks (the method I personally used, disk purchasable here)
  • FreeDVDBoot
  • Mod-chipping

Downloading your BIOS

When it comes to downloading BIOS from the Internet, it is illegal since the BIOS is an intellectual property that belongs to Sony. Realistically, you can easily find all the PS2 BIOS on the internet distributed by websites or cloud platforms. Act at your own risk.

PS2 ROM

Obviously, you’ll also need games to play. Depending on your country legislation, you can or cannot make a copy of your PS2 game if it is for personal use. If you don’t want to deal with the hassle of copying your game , there’s a way to play PS2 CD’s directly from your computer disk reader.

Assuming that you want to make a copy of your game so you don’t have to constantly put your game on the disk reader, you have to use a disk burner software such as ImgBurn.

I’d like to add that while PCSX2 support 98 % of the PS2 library, there are some unlucky titles that won’t be playable. To check the games that are playable and those who are not, click here.

Installing PCSX2

The first step is to download PCSX2 here. I advise you to download the latest stable release which is, to this day, the 1.6.0 version.

If you are on Windows, you would download the PCSX2 setup executable. Once it’s launched follow these steps.

  • Select the Normal Installation
  • Select your language
  • Leave all the plugins to the defaults one
  • Select your PS2 BIOS

After all these steps, you’ll have a working PS2 with no controls nor game. To check if the PS2 BIOS is good, boot the PS2 by first pressing on CDVD > No disc then click on System > Boot BIOS.

If the emulator boots up, hurray ! All is working as intended.

Configuration
Controller

Now that the emulator is working, you need to map your controller (or keyboard) by using the LilyPad plugin. To access the settings of the plugin, you have to go to Config > Controllers > Plugin Settings.

Once the LilyPad settings are open, click on “Pad 1A” beside the general tab and map your controller. You can use the quick setup method to quickly configure your controller. Once it’s done, apply the settings.

Emulation settings

Now we’ll go over the emulation settings, you’ll need to change couples of things in order to make the gaming experience as smooth as possible. To achieve that, you’ll need to go over Config > Emulations Settings > Speedhacks.

When you’re here, activate MTVU (if your CPU has more than 3 cores). There’s a little slider on the bottom, set it to balanced and apply the settings. You might want to set the slider to agressive mode if your CPU is underperforming.

If you face any instability when playing a certain game (frame drop, weird textures), I advise you to do these two things in this order. First, modify the preset level to a less agressive level. If it’s not helping, then disable MTVU as a last resort.

Loading your game

Loading your game is easy as ABC, open PCSX2 and click on CDVD. Now choose the source of your ISO (HDD, diskreader) and it’s done ! You’re playing PS2 game on your computer, brilliant isn’t it?

Conclusion

There’s nothing that replaces the authentic feeling of turning on a real Playstation 2, but the comfort of being able to play on your setup is always appreciated. I hope this article has guided you through the installation of the emulator. Have fun !
